Hennur Road – Evaluating infrastructure and amenities in the vicinity

8th March 2018

Hennur Road is found just outside the Outer Ring road in Bangalore. Hennur lines up in the north of Bangalore alongside major thriving areas of the city like Banaswadi, HBR layout and Hebbal. The areas that come under Hennur are HRBR layout, Kacharakanahalli, Hormavu and Agara, Ganga Nagar and Ashoka Nagar.The zone is very quiet and just a couple of years back was thought to be a part of the outskirts of the city. In any case, with the extensive real estate blast, the entire region has transformed into an urban town housing the most prestigious schools, universities, banks, shopping centres and different amenities that are basic for having a decent lifestyle.

Hennur Road is well connected to the core of Bangalore city and is situated within a short distance from the International Airport. It is just around 8 km from the well-known MG street and the closest railway station of Banaswadi is situated at just 20 km. Although, there is no immediate metro availability to Hennur, the closest Metro station at Baypannahalli is within 7 km distance. The place is additionally served by BMTC transport facility travelling to various parts of the town with a high frequency of both government and private vehicles. A modest bunch of flyovers are likewise under development to additionally enhance the availability of the area and make travelling a smooth and bother free experience.

Hennur Road has pulled in the most famous names in the corporate world. It is situated at a short distance from Manyata Tech Park, one of the greatest IT centres in Bangalore. Various MNCs and business organizations have set up workplaces in the area and most of them include companies like Jlpan Infotech, Philips Software Center, IBM, Hewlett Packard and Emmosys Technologies Private Limited, and so on. It is a perfect place for individuals searching for a place to stay that isn't too far from their work environment.

 

Residential real estate has seen a recent spurt of growth due to numerous factors like connectivity and simple logistics. It has great connectivity to the International airport and the Outer Ring road. The Hebbal main road has good connectivity to Hebbal, Cox town, Frazer town and MG road.

 

The route playing a significant role in connectivity is Hennur Main Road and there is also railway service making it an ideal spot to settle. The nearest Railroad track is Banaswadi Railway station, although the city railway station is at 20km distance. Cafe Moksh, Adiga's Restaurant, Yuan Restaurant, Little Imperial Restaurant are famous restaurants of this place. For music and dance lovers and those who have the willingness to learn this artistic creation, for Pianos & Music Universal Tuners, Indian Institute of Music and Arts will be the right choice to flavour their desire. For recreational purpose, the nearest clubs to Hennur are Country Club India Limited and Rotary Club of Bangalore Camp

Upcoming Infrastructure in Hennur:

 

With all these facilities available at your disposal, Hennur is yet to see various other infrastructures in the future. The flyover via Hennur towards Geddalahalli is planned to be open to the public in October 2018. In the next 5 years, Hennur will witness a boom in residential projects offering world class amenities and luxury living, all under one roof. Lavish homes with excellent quality, features and specification will make Hennur a perfect spot to settle down.

 

As future initiatives, the Hennur main road will be widened from 80 ft to 120 ft as a measure to solve the congestion that occurs during peak hours. Hennur main road is the heart of connectivity to various major roads like MG Road, Cantonment, Hebbal etc. Hence, widening it will only make the daily travel easier.

 

Yet another initiative is the 33 km high speed rail link connecting the city centre to the airport. Despite the proposed road widening plan, the rail link will provide a direct connectivity to the airport, avoiding all the hustle and bustle of a road.

 

A 116 km peripheral ring road has also been proposed to connect Hennur Road, Old Madras Road, Bellary road and other such areas around the vicinity. Therefore, a single road to connect all the major areas will be implemented to avoid unnecessary detours.

 

Apart from the already existing purple and green metro line, Namma Metro Phase II will be initiated where a 72 km metro line running from Nagavara to Gottigere. Nagavara is only 5 km from Hennur main road, providing and easy journey throughout the day.

 

Hennur will soon be the hub for all IT companies with the state-of-the-art International Convention Centre equipped enough to host various domestic and international business clientele. Also, an ITIR or the Information Technology Information Region has been proposed to provide 4 million direct or indirect employment, making Hennur the next hottest destination for those looking for a job. The Devanahalli Business Park ranging over 400 acres will be a significant addition to the IT boom around Hennur.

 

Stated to be the new business district in Bangalore, Hennur will be an added benefit to your list of reason to invest here.
